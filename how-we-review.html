Methodology

How we review AI tools

Every review on AIToolGrade follows the same rigorous process. Here's exactly who does the reviewing, how we evaluate tools, how we score them, and what our grades mean.

Who writes our reviews

AIToolGrade was founded by Rich Nashawaty, an SEO professional with 15 years of experience. Reviews are written by the AIToolGrade editorial team — practitioners who use AI tools in their actual work across writing, software development, automation, and design. We don't assign reviews to people unfamiliar with the category.

Where AI tools are used to assist with research or initial drafts, that content is always reviewed, verified, and rewritten by a human editor before publication. Our scores, verdicts, and recommendations are always human judgments based on direct tool use.

Our review process

1

30-day hands-on testing

We use every tool for a minimum of 30 days across real tasks before writing a single word of our review. That means actual projects — not curated demos designed to make the tool look good.

2

Real-world use cases

We test each tool against the tasks our readers actually care about. For writing tools that means long-form content, ad copy, and email. For coding tools it means real debugging sessions, code generation, and refactoring. Generic benchmarks don't tell you what daily use feels like — we do.

3

Competitor comparison

We run the same prompts and tasks through competing tools so our scores are relative, not just absolute. A tool that scores 8.4 on AIToolGrade is genuinely better than a 7.9 in the same category.

4

Pricing verification

We verify all pricing directly from the product's official website at the time of publication and note any limitations on free plans, trial periods, or hidden costs.

5

Regular updates

AI tools change fast — sometimes dramatically. We revisit reviews when major updates ship and update scores when a product has meaningfully improved or declined. Publication and last-updated dates are shown on every review.

How we score

Each tool is scored out of 10 across five categories. The overall score is a weighted average based on what matters most in that tool category.

Scoring criteria

Output Quality
How good is the actual output? Does it require heavy editing or is it usable as-is? This carries the most weight in our scoring.
Ease of Use
How quickly can a new user get value? Is the interface intuitive without requiring a tutorial to get started?
Value for Money
Is the pricing fair relative to what you get? How does it compare to alternatives at a similar price point?
Features
Does the tool have the features you need? Are they well-implemented or just checkbox items that don't work well in practice?
Support
How responsive is customer support? Is the documentation adequate for both beginners and advanced users?

Our use of AI in the review process

We believe in being transparent about this. AIToolGrade uses AI tools — including some of the tools we review — to assist with research, data gathering, and initial drafts. This is part of how we test them: by using them in a real editorial workflow.

All published content is written, edited, and verified by human reviewers. AI-assisted research is always cross-referenced against primary sources. Scores, verdicts, and recommendations are entirely human judgments. We never publish AI-generated content without substantive human review and editing.

Our editorial independence

AIToolGrade earns revenue through affiliate commissions. Some links on our site may earn us a fee if you sign up for a tool. This never influences our scores or recommendations — we have declined affiliate arrangements with tools that didn't meet our standards, and we have given low scores to tools that do have affiliate programs.

No company can pay for a better score or a featured placement in our rankings. Period.
