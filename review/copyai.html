What is Copy.ai?

Copy.ai is not the same product it was 12 months ago. In October 2025, Copy.ai was acquired by Fullcast and rebranded internally as Fullcast Propel. The original template-based copywriting tool still exists, but the product focus has fully shifted to Workflows — an automation system that chains AI tasks together for multi-step marketing and sales processes. If you're evaluating Copy.ai for AI writing, you're evaluating a product that has pivoted away from that as its core focus.

This review covers what Copy.ai is in 2026 — a GTM automation platform with an AI writing layer attached — based on the post-acquisition product, current pricing, and community feedback from the year since the deal closed.

What Copy.ai Was (for context)

Originally launched in 2020, Copy.ai built its early reputation as a template-based AI copywriting tool — headlines, product descriptions, email drafts, social captions. The free tier was generous, the interface was approachable, and for two or three years it was the go-to entry point for solo creators trying AI writing for the first time. Those features still exist. They are not where the company is investing anymore.

What Copy.ai Is Now (2026)

Post-acquisition, the product is positioned as an AI-powered GTM and workflow platform for marketing and sales teams. The features fall into six buckets:

  • Workflows — the strategic centerpiece. Automated multi-step processes that pull leads from a CRM, research each company, draft personalized outreach, and queue everything for human review. This is the feature Copy.ai now leads with.
  • Chat interface — a ChatGPT-style conversation surface for ad hoc generation tasks, sitting inside the same workspace as the templates and workflows.
  • 90+ marketing-specific templates — the original copy templates are still there, covering ads, emails, social, product descriptions, and blog drafts.
  • Brand voice training — upload existing content, define tone guidelines, and have outputs steered toward your established voice. Available on paid plans.
  • Multi-model access — GPT-4, Claude 3, and Gemini are all accessible from the same interface. Useful for teams that want to compare outputs without juggling separate accounts.
  • 25+ language support — broader than most competitors in the AI writing category.

If your evaluation question is "is this still a good AI writing tool," the more accurate question in 2026 is "is this a good GTM automation platform that happens to include AI writing." The answer to the second question is mostly yes. The answer to the first is more complicated.

Workflows in Practice

Because Workflows is the feature the entire product is now organised around, it is worth being concrete about what one actually looks like. A typical Workflow is a chain of steps the platform executes in sequence — some AI, some integrations, some human review. Three examples that come up repeatedly in case studies and community discussion:

  • Outbound prospecting. Pull a list of leads from your CRM, have the system research each company's recent news and funding, draft a personalized cold email referencing what it found, and queue everything in a review inbox before send. The team approves or edits; nothing goes out automatically.
  • Account research briefs. Feed in an account list before a sales QBR, and the Workflow pulls together a one-page brief per account — recent product launches, leadership changes, hiring signals, and a suggested talking point. Saves analysts hours of manual research per cycle.
  • Content repurposing pipelines. Drop a blog post in, and the Workflow generates the LinkedIn version, the X thread, the email newsletter excerpt, and a short-form video script — all in your brand voice, all ready for human polish.

None of this is impossible to assemble manually with Zapier, ChatGPT, and a Google Sheet. The pitch for Workflows is that it is already assembled — pre-built, integrated with the AI layer, and designed for marketing and sales teams rather than developers. That packaging is real value if your team would otherwise spend a sprint building it themselves.

Pricing

Pricing is the area where the post-acquisition shift shows most clearly. The free tier still exists, the enterprise direction is louder than ever, and the middle tier — historically the most-discussed price point — has been removed from the public site entirely. As of May 2026:

  • Free — $0/month. 2,000 words per month, no credit card required, access to templates and the chat interface. Enough to test the product but not enough for daily use.
  • Pro — pricing not publicly listed. Requires contacting sales. This is a clear shift from the previous $36/month public tier and a transparency issue worth flagging — most direct competitors still publish Pro pricing on the marketing page.
  • Enterprise — custom. Full Workflows, CRM integrations, brand voice training, team management, usage analytics. The plan the company is built around.

The removal of public Pro pricing tells you what the company is selling now: enterprise GTM contracts, not seat-based subscriptions for individuals. Whether that is a problem depends entirely on which side of the buying table you sit on.

What Changed

Copy.ai was acquired by Fullcast in October 2025 and rebranded internally as Fullcast Propel. The product has pivoted from an AI writing tool to a GTM automation platform. Pro pricing has been removed from the public pricing page — sales contact now required. Free tier still available at 2,000 words/month.

Who Copy.ai Is For in 2026

The pivot makes the audience question much easier to answer. The answer is just different than it was a year ago.

Best for:

  • Marketing and sales teams running high-volume outreach and content pipelines
  • Teams that want to automate multi-step GTM workflows, not just generate copy
  • Enterprise teams with CRM integration needs and budget for custom contracts

Not for:

  • Solo creators or freelancers looking for a simple AI writing tool
  • Anyone on a tight budget — Pro pricing is hidden, enterprise focus means SMB pricing is secondary
  • SEO content teams — raw output is generic and requires heavy editing for ranking

The honest read: if you came here looking for the friendly AI copywriting tool Copy.ai was in 2023, you are arriving a couple of product cycles late. The replacement for that role in the category is now Writesonic for individuals or Jasper for small content teams. Copy.ai itself has moved upmarket.

AIToolGrade Take

Copy.ai in 2026 is two products in one — a competent but unremarkable AI writing tool, and an increasingly capable GTM automation platform. The Workflows feature is the strongest differentiator in the AI writing category and represents real value for marketing and sales teams running at scale. The 1.9/5 Trustpilot score is worth taking seriously — it suggests a meaningful gap between how enterprise teams experience the product and how individual users do. The acquisition by Fullcast also raises a question worth watching: is Copy.ai being built as a standalone product, or as a component of a larger Fullcast sales platform? For solo creators and small teams, Writesonic or Jasper remain more transparent and better-priced options.

The Bottom Line

Copy.ai in 2026 is a different product than the one most reviews still describe. The Fullcast acquisition reshaped the roadmap, the Workflows feature reshaped the pitch, and the removal of public Pro pricing reshaped who the company is talking to. For marketing and sales teams looking to automate multi-step GTM processes with AI in the loop, it is one of the more interesting tools in the category — and the Workflows differentiator is real.

For the audience that made Copy.ai popular in the first place — solo creators, freelancers, and small teams looking for a friendly AI writing tool with transparent pricing — the answer has changed. Writesonic and Jasper now serve that segment better. The Trustpilot 1.9/5 score is a credible signal that the post-acquisition support and billing experience for individual users still needs work, and until that catches up, anyone outside the enterprise sweet spot should test the free tier carefully before committing.

The honest summary: Copy.ai went upmarket, and the score reflects the shift. It is a stronger product for some buyers and a weaker fit for others. Match the tool to the role you actually need it to play.
